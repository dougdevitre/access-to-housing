# Glossary

Plain-language definitions of key terms used across Access to Housing modules.
Designed for housing advocates, community members, and first-time users.

---

## Housing & Real Estate

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **ACS (American Community Survey)** | Annual Census Bureau survey that provides community-level data on demographics, income, housing costs, and commuting patterns. Primary source for rent burden and household income data. |
| **Absorption Rate** | How quickly available homes are being sold or rented in a market. High absorption = homes selling fast. Low absorption = homes sitting on market. |
| **ADU (Accessory Dwelling Unit)** | A secondary housing unit on a single-family lot — sometimes called a "granny flat," "in-law suite," or "backyard cottage." |
| **Affordability Index** | A measure of whether a typical family can afford a typical home. Often expressed as a ratio of median home price to median household income. |
| **AVM (Automated Valuation Model)** | A computer-generated estimate of a property's value based on comparable sales, tax records, and market data. Not an appraisal. |
| **Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate)** | Annual rental income divided by property price. Higher cap rate = higher potential return (but often higher risk). Used by investors to compare properties. |
| **CDFI (Community Development Financial Institution)** | A lender focused on providing credit and financial services to underserved communities. Certified by the U.S. Treasury. |
| **Community Land Trust (CLT)** | A nonprofit that owns land and leases it to homeowners at affordable rates, keeping housing permanently affordable even as land values rise. |
| **Comprehensive Plan** | A city or county's long-term plan for land use, housing, transportation, and growth. Zoning decisions should be consistent with it. |
| **DOM (Days on Market)** | How long a property has been listed for sale. Rising DOM = cooling market. Falling DOM = heating market. |
| **Eviction Filing Rate** | The number of eviction cases filed per renting household. Higher rates signal housing instability. Data available from Eviction Lab. |
| **GCI (Gross Commission Income)** | Total commission earned by a real estate agent before expenses and splits. |
| **HPI (House Price Index)** | A measure of how home prices change over time. FHFA and Case-Shiller are the two most widely cited indices. |
| **LIHTC (Low-Income Housing Tax Credit)** | A federal tax incentive that encourages private developers to build affordable rental housing. Credits expire after 15–30 years. |
| **MLS (Multiple Listing Service)** | A database used by real estate agents to share property listings. The primary source for active, pending, and sold home data. |
| **Months of Supply** | Current inventory divided by the monthly sales rate. Under 4 months = seller's market. Over 6 months = buyer's market. |
| **NOI (Net Operating Income)** | Rental income minus operating expenses (but before mortgage payments). Key metric for investment property analysis. |
| **Rent Burden** | The percentage of household income spent on rent. Over 30% = rent-burdened. Over 50% = severely rent-burdened. |
| **Section 8 (Housing Choice Voucher)** | A federal program that helps low-income families pay rent in private-market housing. Administered by local housing authorities. |
| **TIF (Tax Increment Financing)** | A public financing tool that uses future property tax revenue increases to fund current infrastructure improvements in a designated area. |

## Zoning & Land Use

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **By-Right Development** | Construction that is allowed under existing zoning without special approval. Faster and more predictable than discretionary review. |
| **Moratorium** | A temporary halt on certain types of development, demolition, or rent increases. Often used during policy review. |
| **Rezoning** | Changing the zoning designation of a parcel to allow different uses (e.g., single-family to multifamily). Requires government approval. |
| **Variance** | An exception to zoning rules granted to a specific property (e.g., building closer to the property line than the code normally allows). |
| **WUI (Wildland-Urban Interface)** | The area where residential development meets undeveloped wildland. High wildfire risk. Mapped by CAL FIRE and USFS. |

## Climate & Risk

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **FEMA FIRM (Flood Insurance Rate Map)** | Official maps showing flood risk zones. Properties in AE or VE zones typically require flood insurance. |
| **First Street Foundation** | A nonprofit that provides property-level flood, fire, heat, and wind risk scores (1–10 scale). Free at firststreet.org. |
| **Heat Island Effect** | Urban areas that are significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas due to buildings, pavement, and reduced vegetation. |
| **NFIP (National Flood Insurance Program)** | The federal program that provides flood insurance to homeowners, renters, and businesses. Managed by FEMA. |
| **Resilience** | A community's ability to prepare for, withstand, and recover from climate events. Includes infrastructure, insurance, and governance. |
| **SLR (Sea Level Rise)** | The gradual increase in ocean levels due to climate change. NOAA models project 0.3m to 2.0m of rise by 2100 depending on emissions. |

## Civic & Community Trust

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **311 System** | A municipal service request system where residents report non-emergency issues (potholes, streetlights, code violations). Data often available on open data portals. |
| **Code Enforcement** | The municipal function that inspects properties for violations of building, housing, and safety codes. Response times vary widely. |
| **Displacement** | When existing residents are forced to move due to rising costs, redevelopment, or policy changes — not by choice. Measured through rent burden, eviction rates, and investor activity. |
| **FOIA (Freedom of Information Act)** | Federal law requiring government agencies to release records upon request. Every state has a similar law (Sunshine Law, Open Records Act, etc.). |
| **Gentrification** | The process of neighborhood change where rising property values and new investment improve conditions but may price out existing residents. Related to but not the same as displacement. |
| **Open Meetings Act** | State laws requiring government bodies to conduct business in public. Meeting agendas must be posted in advance (typically 24–72 hours). |
| **Public Comment** | The period during a government meeting when residents can speak about agenda items. Written comments typically carry equal weight to oral testimony. |
| **Right of First Refusal** | A policy giving existing tenants or community organizations the first opportunity to purchase a property before it's sold to outside buyers. |

## Data & Methodology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Confidence Level** | How certain the analysis is. High = strong data support. Medium = reasonable but limited data. Low = framework only, data gaps exist. |
| **Density-Normalized** | Rates calculated per population or housing unit (e.g., complaints per 1,000 households) rather than raw counts. Prevents unfair comparison between large and small areas. |
| **Proxy Metric** | A measurable indicator used as a stand-in for something that can't be directly measured. In Fair Housing context, a proxy that correlates with protected class characteristics is prohibited. |
| **Signal** | A data point or trend that indicates a likely future outcome. Stronger when multiple signals converge. |
| **Vintage** | The date or time period a data point was collected or published. Always cited so users can assess currency and verify. |
