# Sample Output: Platform Brief

This is a **template example** showing what a complete multi-module Platform Brief looks like
when the skill produces output. All data points are illustrative — they demonstrate structure
and format, not real-time market conditions. In production, the skill cites actual sources
with vintage dates.

---

## Access to Housing Platform Brief — Nashville, TN Metro — 2026-04-05

**Bottom Line**: Nashville is a high-conviction investment market with strong demand fundamentals,
but infrastructure-driven appreciation in East Nashville and The Nations carries elevated
displacement risk for existing residents. Pair investment analysis with community impact
assessment before committing capital.

**Conviction Level**: Medium — Strong demand signals offset by affordability pressure and
accelerating displacement in key submarkets.

**Key Signals Converging**:
- Module 2 (Permits): Permit pipeline accelerating — 4,200 MF units in pipeline, +18% YoY
- Module 4 (Livability): East Nashville scores 74/100 (Good), up from 68 three years ago
- Module 5 (Infrastructure): $2.1B transit investment in WeGo Star expansion signals 5–7yr value lift
- Module 6 (Climate): Nashville is a net migration destination (+12,400/yr from coastal FL and TX)
- Community Trust (Displacement): East Nashville displacement risk 71/100 (Elevated), accelerating

**Primary Risk**: Affordable housing loss — 1,200 LIHTC units expiring 2027–2029 in Davidson County
with no replacement pipeline identified.

### Module 2: Construction Permit Intelligence — Nashville Metro

**Signal Summary**: Permit pipeline is accelerating with a strong multifamily tilt, signaling
12–24 months of new supply that will partially offset demand pressure.

**Key Findings**:
- 4,200 multifamily units in active pipeline (+18% YoY) — Source: Census BPS, Q4 2025
- SFR permits flat at ~3,100/yr — Source: Census BPS, Q4 2025
- Geographic clustering in The Nations, East Nashville, and Antioch corridors
- Developer concentration moderate — top 3 developers hold 35% of MF pipeline

**Detailed Analysis**:
Filing trend is accelerating, driven by multifamily permits in three primary growth corridors.
The shift from SFR to MF (now 58% of pipeline vs. 44% two years ago) reflects developer
thesis change toward rental product in a market where median home price ($445K, Zillow ZHVI
Dec 2025) has outpaced median household income growth.

Completion lag suggests ~2,800 of these MF units deliver in 12 months, ~4,200 in 24 months.
Absorption rate of 92% (CoStar Q3 2025) suggests the market can absorb this supply, though
a slowdown in job growth could shift the balance.

**Data Caveats**:
- Permit data lags actual construction starts by 1–3 months
- ADU permits not consistently tracked in Davidson County
- Developer financing conditions could delay or cancel permitted projects

**Next Steps**:
- [ ] Monitor quarterly permit filings for acceleration/deceleration signals
- [ ] Track developer financing — rising rates could stall MF pipeline
- [ ] Cross-reference with Module 5 infrastructure investment for growth zone validation

### Displacement Early Warning — East Nashville

**Signal Summary**: Displacement risk is Elevated (71/100) and accelerating — investment that's
improving livability is simultaneously pricing out existing renters.

**Displacement Risk Score**: 71/100 — **Elevated**

| Dimension | Score | Key Driver |
|-----------|:-----:|-----------|
| Affordability Pressure | 24/30 | 52% of renters spending >30% on housing, rent growing 2.3x faster than income |
| Tenure Instability | 18/25 | Eviction filing rate 1.4x metro avg, renter share 64% |
| Investment Pressure | 19/25 | 31% of transactions to non-owner-occupants, cash purchases at 28% |
| Policy Protection | 10/20 | No rent stabilization, no just-cause eviction, no right of first refusal |

**Trend**: Accelerating (+10) — all four dimensions worsening over 3 years

**Converging Signals**:
- Rent burden: 52% of renters cost-burdened, up from 41% in 2022 — Source: Census ACS 2024
- Investor activity: Non-owner-occupant purchases rose from 22% to 31% in 3 years — Source: Property records, Davidson County
- Affordable unit loss: 340 LIHTC units expiring 2027 with no replacement — Source: NLIHC

**Protective Factors**: None identified at the municipal level. No rent stabilization,
just-cause eviction, or community land trust operating in this submarket.

**Data Caveats**:
- Eviction filing data from Eviction Lab (2023 vintage, 12-month lag)
- Investor purchase share estimated from property records; LLC purchases may undercount
- Informal displacement (leaving before eviction) not captured in any dataset

**Next Steps**:
- [ ] Cross-reference with Module 5 — WeGo Star expansion overlaps with highest displacement zones
- [ ] Identify expiring LIHTC contracts in Davidson County (2027–2029)
- [ ] Connect with Nashville Community Land Trust for preservation opportunities
- [ ] Review Metro Council agenda for any pending tenant protection ordinances

### Government Accountability Scorecard — Nashville / Davidson County

**Signal Summary**: Nashville scores 62/100 (Needs Improvement) — permit processing is efficient
but code enforcement response times are lagging and public meeting accessibility is limited.

**Overall Score**: 62/100 — **Needs Improvement**

| Dimension | Score | Trend (3yr) | Key Metric |
|-----------|:-----:|:-----------:|-----------|
| Code Enforcement | 12/25 | down | Avg 47 days violation-to-resolution (peer avg: 28) |
| Permit Processing | 19/25 | up | Avg 21 days residential approval (peer avg: 30) |
| Complaint Resolution | 17/25 | flat | 311 response: 62% resolved within 30 days |
| Public Accessibility | 14/25 | flat | No virtual participation, English-only agendas |

**Peer Comparison**: Nashville's code enforcement lags peer cities (Austin: 24 days, Raleigh: 31
days). Permit processing is above average. 311 resolution rate is in line with peers.

**Equity Check**: 311 response times are 1.6x longer in North Nashville compared to Green Hills —
infrastructure complaints in lower-income areas take significantly longer to resolve.

**Budget Signal**: Housing and community development represents 2.1% of Metro Nashville's
operating budget ($48.3M, FY2025). Per-capita housing spend: $68 (peer avg: $82).

**Data Caveats**:
- Code enforcement data from Metro Nashville open data portal (2025 vintage)
- 311 response times self-reported by department; actual resolution may differ from "closed" status
- Public accessibility assessment based on 2025 meeting review; may not reflect recent changes

## How to Read This Output

| Section | Purpose | Developer Note |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| **Bottom Line** | One decision the user can act on | Map to `PlatformBrief.bottomLine` |
| **Conviction Level** | Confidence calibration | Map to `PlatformBrief.convictionLevel` |
| **Key Signals** | Cross-module evidence | Map to `PlatformBrief.keySignalsConverging[]` |
| **Primary Risk** | Single biggest downside | Map to `PlatformBrief.primaryRisk` |
| **Module Sections** | Detailed analysis per module | Map to `PlatformBrief.modules[]` — use individual schemas |
| **Data Caveats** | What we don't know | Always present — absence of caveats is a red flag |
| **Next Steps** | Actionable follow-ups | Checkboxes for user tracking |

All data points in production outputs include **source name** and **vintage date**.
Illustrative data in this sample is marked with source and approximate vintage for format reference.
